Max-plus linear (MPL) systems

Discrete-event systems modelled using (max,+) algebra

Example: production system

  • 3 production units \(P_1, P_2, P_3\),
  • \(P_3\) waits for the outputs from \(P_1\) and \(P_2\),
  • processing delays \(v_1 = 12, v_2 = 11, v_3 = 7\),
  • transportation delays: \(d_2 = 2\) from the entry to \(P_2\), \(d_4 = 1\) from \(P_2\) to \(P_3\), the rest is negligible.

Timed Petri net (event graph) for the example

Simplified Petri net (delayed places)

State equations for the example

\[ \begin{aligned} x_{1,k} &= \max\{x_{1,k-1} + 12, u_k + 0\}\\ x_{2,k} &= \max\{x_{2,k-1} + 11, u_k + 2\}\\ x_{3,k} &= \max\{x_{3,k-1} + 7, x_{1,k} +12 + 0, x_{2,k} + 11 + 1\}\\ &= \max\{x_{3,k-1} + 7, \max\{x_{1,k-1} + 12, u_k\} +12, \\ &\qquad \max\{x_{2,k-1} + 11, u_k+2\} + 12\}\\ &= \max\{x_{3,k-1} + 7, x_{1,k-1} + 24, x_{2,k-1} + 23, \\ &\qquad\qquad u_k+14\}\\ y_k &= x_{3,k} + 7 \end{aligned} \]

State equations in (max,+) algebra

\[ \begin{aligned} \begin{bmatrix} x_{1,k} \\ x_{2,k} \\ x_{3,k} \end{bmatrix} &= \begin{bmatrix} 12 & \varepsilon & \varepsilon\\ \varepsilon & 11 & \varepsilon\\ 24 & 23 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \otimes \begin{bmatrix} x_{1,k-1} \\ x_{2,k-1} \\ x_{3,k-1} \end{bmatrix} \oplus \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 2 \\ 14 \end{bmatrix} \otimes u_k\\ y_k &= \begin{bmatrix} \varepsilon & \varepsilon & 7 \end{bmatrix} \otimes x_k \end{aligned} \]

Model or an event graph as a Max-plus linear (MPL) state-space system

\[ \begin{aligned} x(k) &= A\otimes x(k-1) \oplus B\otimes u(k),\\ y(k) &= C\otimes x(k) \end{aligned} \]

  • or, equivalently (after relabelling) \[ \begin{aligned} x(k+1) &= A\otimes x(k) \oplus B\otimes u(k),\\ y(k) &= C\otimes x(k) \end{aligned} \]

which mimics the conventional state-space system \[ \begin{aligned} x(k+1) &= A x(k) + Bu(k),\\ y(k) &= Cx(k) \end{aligned} \]

  • But the role of the variables \(u(k), x(k), y(k)\) is that they are event times, namely times of
    • arrivals of inputs,
    • beginning of processing
    • finishing of processing, respectively.
  • The independent variable \(k\) is now a counter of the events.

Simulation of an MPL system

\[ \begin{aligned} x_1 &= A\otimes x_0 \oplus B\otimes u_1\\ x_2 &= A\otimes x_1 \oplus B\otimes u_2\\ &= A\otimes (A\otimes x_0 \oplus B\otimes u_1) \oplus B\otimes u_2\\ &= A^{\otimes^2}\otimes x_0 \oplus A\otimes B\otimes u_1 \oplus B\otimes u_2\\ &\vdots \end{aligned} \]

. . .

\[\boxed{ x_k = A^{\otimes^k}\otimes x_0 \oplus \bigoplus_{i=1}^k A^{\otimes^{k-i}} \otimes B\otimes u_i} \]

Note how the lower and upper bounds for the summation are shifted by 1 compared to the traditional convolution.

Linear, indeed, but in (max,+) sense

  • two input sequences \(u_1= \{u_{1,1},u_{1,2},\ldots\}\) and \(u_2= \{u_{2,1},u_{2,2},\ldots\}\),
  • (max,+)-linear combination \(\alpha \otimes u_1 \oplus \beta \otimes u_2\) of the two inputs
  • gives the same (max,+)-linear combination of the outputs \(y_1\) and \(y_2\).

Input-output response – eliminating the state variables

\[ Y = G\otimes x_0 \oplus H\otimes U \]

. . .

\[ H = \begin{bmatrix} C\otimes B & \varepsilon & \varepsilon & \ldots & \varepsilon\\ C\otimes A\otimes B & C\otimes B & \varepsilon & \ldots & \varepsilon\\ C\otimes A^{\otimes^2}\otimes B & C\otimes A\otimes B & C\otimes B & \ldots & \varepsilon\\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots\\ C\otimes A^{\otimes^{p-1}}\otimes B & C\otimes A^{\otimes^{p-2}}\otimes B & C\otimes A^{\otimes^{p-3}}\otimes B & \ldots & C\otimes B \end{bmatrix} \]

. . .

\[ G = \begin{bmatrix} C \\ C\otimes A \\ C\otimes A^{\otimes^2} \\ \vdots \\ C\otimes A^{\otimes^{p-1}} \end{bmatrix} \]

The capital U and Y are sequences composed of the “samples” of u and y.

Example (the production system)

\[ Y = \begin{bmatrix}y_1 & y_2 & y_3 & y_4\end{bmatrix}^\top, \quad U = \begin{bmatrix}u_1 & u_2 & u_3 & u_4\end{bmatrix}^\top \]

. . .

\[ x_0 = \begin{bmatrix}\varepsilon & \varepsilon & \varepsilon\end{bmatrix}^\top \]

. . .

\[ H = \begin{bmatrix} 21 & \varepsilon & \varepsilon & \varepsilon\\ 32 & 21 & \varepsilon & \varepsilon\\ 43 & 32 & 21 & \varepsilon\\ 55 & 43 & 32 & 21 \end{bmatrix} \]

Analysis of an irreducible MPL system

Autonomous MPL system \[ x_{k+c} = A^{\otimes^{k+c}}\otimes x_0 \]

. . .

For large enough \(k\) and \(c\) \[ x_{k+c} = \lambda^{\otimes^c}\otimes A^{\otimes^{k}}\otimes x_0 = \lambda^{\otimes^c}\otimes x_k \]

. . .

\[ x_{k+c}-x_k = c\lambda \]

  • When the production system reaches a cyclic behaviour, the average cycle is \(\lambda\). The average production rate is \(1/\lambda\).

Cosf function fo MPC

\[ J = J_\mathrm{output} + \lambda J_\mathrm{input} \]

. . .

At “time” \(k\), with the prediction horizon \(N_\mathrm{p}\), with \(n_\mathrm{y}\): \[ J_\mathrm{output} = \sum_{j=0}^{N_\mathrm{p}-1}\sum_{i=1}^{n_\mathrm{y}} \max \{y_{i,k+j} - r_{i,k+j},0\} \]

  • penalizes tardiness (late delivery)

Is the lower value for j correct?

Alternative output costs

. . .

\[ J_\mathrm{output} = \sum_{j=0}^{N_\mathrm{p}-1}\sum_{i=1}^{n_\mathrm{y}} \left|y_{i,k+j} - r_{i,k+j} \right | \]

  • penalizes difference between the due and actual dates.

. . .

\[ J_\mathrm{output} = \sum_{j=1}^{N_\mathrm{p}-1}\sum_{i=1}^{n_\mathrm{y}} \left |\Delta^2 y_{i,k+j}\right | \]

  • balances the output rates.

Input costs

\[ J_\mathrm{input} = -\sum_{j=0}^{N_\mathrm{p}-1}\sum_{l=1}^{n_\mathrm{u}} u_{l,k+j} \]

  • penalizes early feeding (favours just-in-time feeding).
  • Note the minus sign.

Control horizon vs. prediction horizon

  • Assume constant feeding rate after the control horizon \(N_\mathrm{c}\)

. . .

\[ \Delta u_{k+j} = \Delta u_{k+N_\mathrm{c}-1},\qquad j=N_\mathrm{c},\ldots, N_\mathrm{p}-1 \] where \(\Delta u_k = u_k - u_{k-1}\).

  • or \[ \Delta^2 u_{k+j} = 0,\qquad j=N_\mathrm{c},\ldots, N_\mathrm{p}-1 \] where \(\Delta^2 u_k = \Delta u_k - \Delta u_{k-1} = u_k - 2u_{k-1} + u_{k-2}\)

Inequality constraints for MPC

Minimum and maximum separation of input and output events

. . .

\[ a_{k+j} \leq \Delta u_{k+j} \leq b_{k+j},\qquad j=0,1,\ldots,N_\mathrm{c}-1 \]

. . .

\[ c_{k+j} \leq \Delta y_{k+j} \leq d_{k+j},\qquad j=0,1,\ldots,N_\mathrm{p}-1 \]

. . .

Maximum due dates for the output events \[ y_{k+j} \leq r_{k+j},\qquad j=0,1,\ldots,N_\mathrm{p}-1 \]

. . .

Input and output events are consecutive \[ \Delta u_{k+j} \geq 0, \qquad j=0,1,\ldots,N_\mathrm{c}-1. \]

The MPL MPC optimization problem is nonlinear

  • general nonlinear solvers (fmincon, ipopt, …)
  • Extended Linear Complementarity Problem (ELCP) by De Schutter
  • Mixed Integer (Linear) Programming
